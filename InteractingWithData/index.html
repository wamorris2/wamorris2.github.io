Interacting With Data

Parallel Categories Graph

The visualization shows that about 30% of people who never vote, 40% of people who sporadically vote, and 50% of people who always vote go to college, showing a clear upwards trend in that regard. The income bracket where people who rarely/never vote make up the largest percentage is the "less than $40k" bracket. They are also most likely to only have only a high school diploma or less. On the other hand, of the people who go to college, most end up in a higher income bracket. This is shown in the visualization as thicker and thicker lines stemming from the college bracket as you move down (higher) in the income category.



Bubble Graph

The election in 2020 has a different shape than all the others. Its percentage of absentee voters was dramatically higher than any other year, with one county having 85% of voters being absentee voters. The main reason this is the case is that the Covid-19 pandemic was in full swing at the time, and, combined with a presidential election, a lot of people wanted to vote but didn't want to come out of their homes to vote.

The elections in 2016 and 2020 had noticeably higher voter turnout compared to the other years. They both hung around an average of 80% turnout (estimated by eye) whereas the other years had closer to 50-60% turnout.



Choropleth

Counties with higher voter turnout tend to be in Northern and Eastern Virginia while Southern and Western counties tend to turnout less. In addition, in regards to cities within counties, the cities tend to have a lower turnout than the counties they are in, possibly due to the presence of the suburbs.
